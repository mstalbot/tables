de Toulouse, CNRS, 57 avenue d'Azereix, 65000 Tarbes, France \n      <br>\n        <sup>4</sup> \n        Dark Cosmology Centre, Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen, Juliane Marie Vej 30, 2100 Copenhagen, Denmark \n      <br>\n        <sup>5</sup> \n        Laboratoire d'Astrophysique de Marseille, OAMP, CNRS-Universit&#233; Aix-Marseille, 38 rue Fr&#233;d&#233;ric Joliot-Curie, 13388 Marseille Cedex&#160;13, France \n      <br></p>\n<p class=\"history\"><span class=\"bold\">Received: </span>\n          17 \n          November \n          2008\n        <br><span class=\"bold\">Accepted: </span>\n          30 \n          January \n          2009\n        </p>\n<p class=\"bold\"><a name=\"abs\"></a>Abstract</p>\n        <p><i>Context. </i>Strong gravitational lensing is a unique tool that can be used to accurately model the inner mass distribution of massive galaxy clusters. \nIn particular, clusters with large Einstein radii provide a wealth of multiply imaged systems in the cluster core. \nMeasuring the redshift of these multiple images provides strong constraints for precisely determining the shape of the central dark matter profile.</p>\n        <p><i>Aims. </i>This paper presents a spectroscopic survey of strongly lensed galaxies in the massive cluster lens Abell&#160;1703, which displays a large Einstein radius (28&#180;&#180;&#8192;at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq1.png\"></span>) and numerous strongly-lensed systems including a central <i>ring</i>-like configuration.</p>\n        <p><i>Methods. </i>We used the LRIS spectrograph on Keck to target multiple images and lensed galaxy \ncandidates, and used the measured spectroscopic redshifts to constrain the \nmass distribution of the cluster using a parametric model.</p>\n        <p><i>Results. </i>The spectroscopic data enable us to measure accurate redshifts for 7&#160;sources at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq2.png\"></span> , all of which are in good agreement with their photometric redshifts. We update the identification of multiply imaged systems by discovering 3&#160;new systems and identifying a radial counter image. We also report the discovery of a remarkably bright ~3.6&#160;<i>L<sup>*</sup></i> <i>i</i>-band dropout at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq3.png\"></span> in our mask and it is only moderately magnified by the cluster (<span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq4.png\"></span>). The improved parametric mass model, including 16&#160;multiple systems with 10 spectroscopic redshifts, further constrains the smooth cluster-scale mass distribution with a generalized NFW profile of best-fit logarithmic slope <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq5.png\"></span>, concentration <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq6.png\"></span> and scale radius <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq7.png\"></span>&#160;kpc. The overall rms in the image plane is 1.3&#180;&#180;. </p>\n        <p><i>Conclusions. </i>Our strong-lensing model allows us to predict a large-scale shear signal that is consistent with weak-lensing measurements inferred from Subaru data out to 4&#160;Mpc&#160;<i>h</i><sup>-1</sup>. Also considering the strong-lensing modeling requires a single dark matter clump, \nthis suggests that Abell&#160;1703 is a relaxed, unimodal cluster. This unique cluster could be probed further using deep X-ray, SZ, and dynamics analysis for a detailed study of the physics in a relaxed cluster.</p>\n      <div class=\"kword\"><p><span class=\"bold\">Key words: </span>galaxies : high-redshift / galaxies: distances and redshifts / galaxies: clusters: individual: Abell&#160;1703 / gravitational lensing</p></div>\n</div>\n<p><i>&#169; ESO, 2009</i></p>\n</div>\n\r\n</div>\r\n\r\n    <div id=\"metrics-tabs\" data-doi=\"10.1051/0004-6361/200811366\" data-edps_ref=\"aa11366-08\">\n<nav class=\"toolbar\">\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"metrics-siq\">Current usage metrics</button>\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"info\">About article metrics</button>\n    \n\t\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"return\">Return to article</button>\n</nav>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-siq\">\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-alm\"></div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"info\">\n\t\n\n\t<p>Current usage metrics show cumulative count of Article Views (full-text article views including HTML views, PDF and ePub downloads, according to the available data)  and Abstracts Views on Vision4Press platform.</p>\n\n\t<p>Data correspond to usage on the plateform after 2015. Aix-Marseille, 38 rue Fr&#233;d&#233;ric Joliot-Curie, 13388 Marseille Cedex&#160;13, France \n      <br></p>\n<p class=\"history\"><span class=\"bold\">Received: </span>\n          17 \n          November \n          2008\n        <br><span class=\"bold\">Accepted: </span>\n          30 \n          January \n          2009\n        </p>\n<p class=\"bold\"><a name=\"abs\"></a>Abstract</p>\n        <p><i>Context. </i>Strong gravitational lensing is a unique tool that can be used to accurately model the inner mass distribution of massive galaxy clusters. \nIn particular, clusters with large Einstein radii provide a wealth of multiply imaged systems in the cluster core. \nMeasuring the redshift of these multiple images provides strong constraints for precisely determining the shape of the central dark matter profile.</p>\n        <p><i>Aims. </i>This paper presents a spectroscopic survey of strongly lensed galaxies in the massive cluster lens Abell&#160;1703, which displays a large Einstein radius (28&#180;&#180;&#8192;at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq1.png\"></span>) and numerous strongly-lensed systems including a central <i>ring</i>-like configuration.</p>\n        <p><i>Methods. </i>We used the LRIS spectrograph on Keck to target multiple images and lensed galaxy \ncandidates, and used the measured spectroscopic redshifts to constrain the \nmass distribution of the cluster using a parametric model.</p>\n        <p><i>Results. </i>The spectroscopic data enable us to measure accurate redshifts for 7&#160;sources at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq2.png\"></span> , all of which are in good agreement with their photometric redshifts. We update the identification of multiply imaged systems by discovering 3&#160;new systems and identifying a radial counter image. We also report the discovery of a remarkably bright ~3.6&#160;<i>L<sup>*</sup></i> <i>i</i>-band dropout at <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq3.png\"></span> in our mask and it is only moderately magnified by the cluster (<span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq4.png\"></span>). The improved parametric mass model, including 16&#160;multiple systems with 10 spectroscopic redshifts, further constrains the smooth cluster-scale mass distribution with a generalized NFW profile of best-fit logarithmic slope <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq5.png\"></span>, concentration <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq6.png\"></span> and scale radius <span class=\"img-inline\"><img src=\"/articles/aa/abs/2009/16/aa11366-08/aa11366-08_tex_eq7.png\"></span>&#160;kpc. The overall rms in the image plane is 1.3&#180;&#180;. </p>\n        <p><i>Conclusions. </i>Our strong-lensing model allows us to predict a large-scale shear signal that is consistent with weak-lensing measurements inferred from Subaru data out to 4&#160;Mpc&#160;<i>h</i><sup>-1</sup>. Also considering the strong-lensing modeling requires a single dark matter clump, \nthis suggests that Abell&#160;1703 is a relaxed, unimodal cluster. This unique cluster could be probed further using deep X-ray, SZ, and dynamics analysis for a detailed study of the physics in a relaxed cluster.</p>\n      <div class=\"kword\"><p><span class=\"bold\">Key words: </span>galaxies : high-redshift / galaxies: distances and redshifts / galaxies: clusters: individual: Abell&#160;1703 / gravitational lensing</p></div>\n</div>\n<p><i>&#169; ESO, 2009</i></p>\n</div>\n\r\n</div>\r\n\r\n    <div id=\"metrics-tabs\" data-doi=\"10.1051/0004-6361/200811366\" data-edps_ref=\"aa11366-08\">\n<nav class=\"toolbar\">\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"metrics-siq\">Current usage metrics</button>\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"info\">About article metrics</button>\n    \n\t\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"return\">Return to article</button>\n</nav>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-siq\">\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-alm\"></div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"info\">\n\t\n\n\t<p>Current usage metrics show cumulative count of Article Views (full-text article views including HTML views, PDF and ePub downloads, according to the available data)  and Abstracts Views on Vision4Press platform.</p>\n\n\t<p>Data correspond to usage on the plateform after 2015.
