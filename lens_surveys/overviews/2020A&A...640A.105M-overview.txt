We complement our data set with other monitoring data available in the literature to measure the time delays in 18 systems, among which nine reach a relative precision better than 15% for at least one time delay. The decade-long light curves of 23 lensed systems are presented for the first time. We complement our data set with other monitoring data available in the literature to measure the time delays in 18 systems, among which nine reach a relative precision better than 15% for at least one time delay. To achieve this, we developed an automated version of the curve-shifting toolbox <tt>PyCS</tt> to ensure robust estimation of the time delay in the presence of microlensing, while accounting for the errors due to the imperfect representation of microlensing. We also re-analysed the previously published time delays of RX J1131&#8722;1231 and HE 0435&#8722;1223, by adding six and two new seasons of monitoring, respectively, and confirming the previous time-delay measurements. When the time delay measurement is possible, we corrected the light curves of the lensed images from their time delay and present the difference curves to highlight the microlensing signal contained in the data. To date, this is the largest sample of decade-long lens monitoring data, which is useful to measure <i>H</i><sub>0</sub> and the size of quasar accretion discs with microlensing as well as to study quasar variability.</p>\n<div class=\"kword\"><p><span class=\"bold\">Key words: </span>gravitational lensing: strong / cosmology: observations</p></div>\n<div class=\"note\">\n<hr width=\"30%\" align=\"left\">\n<div>\n<a name=\"FN1\"></a><sup>&#8902;</sup> <p class=\"ligne\">All light curves presented in this paper are only available at the CDS via anonymous ftp to <a href=\"http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/\" target=\"_blank\">cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr</a> (<a href=\"ftp://130.79.128.5\" target=\"_blank\">130.79.128.5</a>) or via <a href=\"http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/viz-bin/cat/J/A+A/640/A105\" target=\"_blank\">http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/viz-bin/cat/J/A+A/640/A105</a></p>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><i>&#169; ESO 2020</i></p>\n</div>\n\r\n</div>\r\n\r\n    <div id=\"metrics-tabs\" data-doi=\"10.1051/0004-6361/202037740\" data-edps_ref=\"aa37740-20\">\n<nav class=\"toolbar\">\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"metrics-siq\">Current usage metrics</button>\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"info\">About article metrics</button>\n    \n\t\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"return\">Return to article</button>\n</nav>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-siq\">\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-alm\"></div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"info\">\n\t\n\n\t<p>Current usage metrics show cumulative count of Article Views (full-text article views including HTML views, PDF and ePub downloads, according to the available data)  and Abstracts Views on Vision4Press platform.</p>\n\n\t<p>Data correspond to usage on the plateform after 2015. The decade-long light curves of 23 lensed systems are presented for the first time. We complement our data set with other monitoring data available in the literature to measure the time delays in 18 systems, among which nine reach a relative precision better than 15% for at least one time delay. To achieve this, we developed an automated version of the curve-shifting toolbox <tt>PyCS</tt> to ensure robust estimation of the time delay in the presence of microlensing, while accounting for the errors due to the imperfect representation of microlensing. We also re-analysed the previously published time delays of RX J1131&#8722;1231 and HE 0435&#8722;1223, by adding six and two new seasons of monitoring, respectively, and confirming the previous time-delay measurements. When the time delay measurement is possible, we corrected the light curves of the lensed images from their time delay and present the difference curves to highlight the microlensing signal contained in the data. To date, this is the largest sample of decade-long lens monitoring data, which is useful to measure <i>H</i><sub>0</sub> and the size of quasar accretion discs with microlensing as well as to study quasar variability.</p>\n<div class=\"kword\"><p><span class=\"bold\">Key words: </span>gravitational lensing: strong / cosmology: observations</p></div>\n<div class=\"note\">\n<hr width=\"30%\" align=\"left\">\n<div>\n<a name=\"FN1\"></a><sup>&#8902;</sup> <p class=\"ligne\">All light curves presented in this paper are only available at the CDS via anonymous ftp to <a href=\"http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/\" target=\"_blank\">cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr</a> (<a href=\"ftp://130.79.128.5\" target=\"_blank\">130.79.128.5</a>) or via <a href=\"http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/viz-bin/cat/J/A+A/640/A105\" target=\"_blank\">http://cdsarc.u-strasbg.fr/viz-bin/cat/J/A+A/640/A105</a></p>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<p><i>&#169; ESO 2020</i></p>\n</div>\n\r\n</div>\r\n\r\n    <div id=\"metrics-tabs\" data-doi=\"10.1051/0004-6361/202037740\" data-edps_ref=\"aa37740-20\">\n<nav class=\"toolbar\">\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"metrics-siq\">Current usage metrics</button>\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"info\">About article metrics</button>\n    \n\t\n\t<button class=\"toolbar-item\" id=\"return\">Return to article</button>\n</nav>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-siq\">\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"metrics-alm\"></div>\n\n<div class=\"panel\" data-for=\"info\">\n\t\n\n\t<p>Current usage metrics show cumulative count of Article Views (full-text article views including HTML views, PDF and ePub downloads, according to the available data)  and Abstracts Views on Vision4Press platform.</p>\n\n\t<p>Data correspond to usage on the plateform after 2015.
